On the right side of the screen is a mirror standing on the ground, and on the left side, there's a beautiful, sweet, youthful real Chinese beauty looking into the mirror. She's standing sideways, and the reflection of her in the mirror turns into a 2D fashionable animated style girl of the same proportions (and not a real person). The animated girl's outfit and hairstyle are exactly the same as the real woman's. Fixed camera angle, with the screen simultaneously showing the real beauty's side profile, the mirror, and the animated girl's front view. The real beauty dances a cute dance in front of the mirror, and the animated girl in the mirror dances exactly the same way as her. As they dance, the animated girl starts to look more and more speechless and impatient—not only do her eyes start drifting sideways randomly, but her movements also become perfunctory. Eventually, she stops dancing altogether, crosses her arms over her chest and stands still, forming a stark contrast with the enthusiastic real person. The real person keeps dancing, but after a while, she gets anxious, quickly steps up to the mirror, bends down and taps the mirror surface with her index finger, her expression a mix of annoyance and helplessness. The animated girl remains standing in place, looking displeased, then bends down to pull up a beige mini-curtain from the bottom of the screen that's as wide as the mirror frame, drawing it down from top to bottom to cover the entire mirror surface. Right in the center of the curtain hangs a small wooden sign with the three big characters "Off Duty" written on it. The real person stands dumbfounded in front of the mirror, her hands slowly dropping to her sides, her face full of shock.
